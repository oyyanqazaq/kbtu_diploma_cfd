## Project Overview

This diploma project is devoted to the integrated study of air pollution dispersion in urban residential areas using a combination of **numerical simulation** and **data-driven analysis**.

The work focuses on modeling the transport and diffusion of pollutants in the urban atmosphere and evaluating their behavior under different environmental and spatial conditions. The project combines **computational fluid dynamics (CFD)**, **mathematical modeling**, and **data analysis methods** to better understand pollution patterns in city environments.

---

## Research Aim

The main aim of this project is to develop an integrated framework for analyzing air pollution dispersion in urban residential areas by combining:

- **numerical simulation based on physical models**
- **data-driven assessment using environmental and observational data**
- **comparative evaluation of pollution behavior in urban conditions**

---

## Objectives

The project includes the following objectives:

1. Study the physical and mathematical principles of air pollution dispersion.
2. Apply numerical simulation methods based on the **Navier–Stokes equations**.
3. Model pollutant transport in an urban residential environment.
4. Analyze environmental and spatial factors influencing pollutant concentration.
5. Use data-driven methods to interpret, validate, and assess simulation results.
6. Compare simulated and observed patterns of air pollution distribution.

---

## Methodology

The project is based on two main components:

### 1. Numerical Simulation
- mathematical modeling of air flow and pollutant transport
- application of CFD principles
- simulation of dispersion processes in urban geometry
- analysis of concentration fields under different conditions

### 2. Data-Driven Assessment
- preprocessing and analysis of environmental data
- evaluation of pollutant concentration patterns
- comparison of numerical and empirical results
- interpretation of trends using data analysis methods
